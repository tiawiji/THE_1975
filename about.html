About the Band

"The 1975" is an English pop-rock band formed in Manchester in 2002. The band members include Matty Healy (lead vocals, rhythm guitar, keyboards), Adam Hann (lead guitar, keyboards), Ross MacDonald (bass guitar, keyboards), and George Daniel (drums, keyboards). The band's music is known for its genre-blending style, incorporating elements of indie rock, pop, electronic, and R&B.

Discography

  • The 1975 (2013): The band's debut self-titled album, released on September 2, 2013, received critical acclaim and introduced the world to their unique sound. It includes tracks like "Sex," "Chocolate," "Robbers," and "Girls."
  • I Like It When You Sleep, for You Are So Beautiful Yet So Unaware of It (2016): Released on February 26, 2016, this album marked a significant shift in the band's musical direction. It explores more experimental and atmospheric sounds while maintaining their signature catchy hooks. Hit songs from this album include "Love Me," "The Sound," "Somebody Else," and "A Change of Heart."
  • A Brief Inquiry into Online Relationships (2018): Released on November 30, 2018, this album further solidified The 1975's reputation as a boundary-pushing band. The album touches on themes of love, modern society, and technology. Notable tracks include "Give Yourself A Try," "It's Not Living (If It's Not With You)," "Love It If We Made It," and "Sincerity Is Scary."
  • Notes on a Conditional Form (2020): This album, released on May 22, 2020, embraced an even more diverse range of musical styles, including experimental electronic and ambient tracks. With 22 songs, it's the band's longest studio album. Highlights include "People," "Frail State of Mind," "If You're Too Shy (Let Me Know)," and "Me & You Together Song."
  • The band's discography is characterized by its willingness to evolve and experiment with their sound, making each album distinct and offering a fresh experience to their fans. Throughout their career, The 1975 has received numerous awards and nominations, including BRIT Awards, NME Awards, and Q Awards, solidifying their position as one of the most influential bands in modern pop-rock music. Their albums have garnered commercial success and critical acclaim, attracting a dedicated and passionate fanbase worldwide.
